About Me

I'm a news associate at The Wall Street Journal. I mainly write about housing, health care delivery and big data, though rarely in combination. I previously worked at the Center for Advancing Health Policy Through Research, a health care research center. You can find my work in The Wall Street Journal, Health Affairs, JAMA, The Boston Globe, Gothamist and WNYC

I graduated in 2025 from Brown Universiy, where I tried seven different majors before settling on statistics and public health. But I spent most of my time on campus at the school newspaper anyway.

I grew up on Long Island and now live in Brooklyn. In my free time, I co-run a free summer research program for high school students.
